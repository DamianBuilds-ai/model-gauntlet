# Decision Brief - Support Platform Standardisation

Company: Cardinal Freight (regional logistics + last-mile delivery, ~140 staff).
Author: Head of Customer Operations.
Date: current quarter.

## The decision

We are consolidating onto ONE customer-support platform for the next three years.
Today support is split across a shared inbox, a spreadsheet escalation tracker, and a
chat tool. It does not scale. We have shortlisted three platforms and must pick one:

- Helmsdesk - managed all-in-one helpdesk (tickets, live chat, knowledge base).
- Quillstack - self-managed open-source support suite (we host and run it).
- Beaconreach - managed platform with a bundled AI assist + analytics layer.

## Constraints that matter to Cardinal

- Support team is SMALL: 4 support agents + 1 ops lead. No dedicated platform
  engineer. Whoever runs the tool runs it on top of their day job.
- We serve customers in the EU and must keep customer contact data in the EU
  (residency is a hard legal line for us, not a preference).
- Peak season triples ticket volume for ~10 weeks a year. The platform has to absorb
  the spike without us hand-holding it.
- Three-year total cost matters, including the people-time to operate it, not just the
  sticker subscription.

## What we want from the analysis

A single recommendation with the reasoning shown. Weigh the requirements (see the
weighted requirements checklist, doc 16), state which option wins and why, name what
ruled the losers out, and flag any source you would not trust. Reconcile anything in
the sources that disagrees rather than quietly picking a side.
